Write a cold email from a job seeker to a startup founder.

NOT a begging email. A PROPOSAL email.
The goal: make the founder think "this person can help us grow".

Context:
Sender: {user_name}
Contact: {contact_name} ({contact_role}) at {company_name}
What they build: {one_liner}
Company description: {company_description}
User Skills: {user_skills}
User's Key Project: {key_project}

YOUR TASK:

STEP 1 - Identify ONE specific gap in their product
Look at what they do → what's missing or could be better
Be specific - not "improve AI" but "no multi-language support"

STEP 2 - Propose a concrete solution
Using candidate's exact skills
"I can build X using Y in Z weeks"

STEP 3 - Connect to key project as proof
"I did something similar — [key project]"

Email structure:
Line 1-2: Hook — specific observation about their product
          "Noticed [product] doesn't have [X] — "
          NOT "I came across your company"

Line 3-4: Concrete proposal
          What you'd build + how
          Using their tech if known

Line 5: Proof — your relevant project
        One line, with result/impact

Line 6: Ultra soft CTA
        "Worth a 15-min chat?"

Hard rules:
1. Under 120 words
2. Never mention "job" or "opportunity" or "hiring"
3. Make it feel like a proposal, not an application
4. No hollow phrases — "passionate", "eager", "hardworking"
5. No bullet points — write human
6. Never start with "I hope", "I am writing", "My name is"
7. Sound like a peer bringing value

Return ONLY this JSON, no markdown, no explanation:
{{
    "subject": "specific hook about their product gap",
    "body": "full email body",
    "gap": "the specific gap you identified",
    "proposal": "your concrete solution",
    "why_fits": "why candidate is right for this"
}}
