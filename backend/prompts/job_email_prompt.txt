Write a job application email that feels like a VALUE ADD, not a request.

Context:
Sender: {user_name}
Recipient: {contact_name} ({contact_role}) at {company_name}
Role applying for: {job_title}
User Skills: {user_skills}
User's Key Project: {key_project}
Experience: {experience_years} years

Job Requires:
{job_description}

Company Context:
{company_summary}
Their tech: {tech_stack}

YOUR TASK:

STEP 1 — Read the job description carefully
Find the CORE problem this role needs to solve.
Not the listed requirements — the actual problem.

STEP 2 — Connect user's project to that problem
"They need X solved — I've already solved something similar"
Be specific — use numbers/results if possible.

STEP 3 — Suggest one small relevant idea
Based on job + company context
Show you've thought about their specific situation
"One thing I'd explore in this role is..."

Email structure:
Line 1-2: Lead with your most relevant work
          NOT your degree or years of experience
          "I recently built X that does Y — [result]"

Line 3-4: Connect to their specific need
          Show you read the JD + understand the company
          "This maps directly to what you're solving with [role]"

Line 5-6: One small idea for the role
          Specific to their product/tech
          Shows initiative + thinking

Line 7: Direct CTA
        "Happy to walk through the project — worth a chat?"

Hard rules:
1. Under 140 words
2. First line = your work, not who you are
3. Idea must use job's tech stack + user's skills
4. No hollow phrases — "passionate", "team player", "fast learner"
5. No bullet points — write human
6. Never start with "I hope", "I am writing", "My name is"
7. Sound like a peer bringing value, not a student asking for a chance

Return ONLY this JSON, nothing else:
{
    "subject": "role name + one specific hook about their work",
    "body": "full email body",
    "core_problem_identified": "what problem the role actually solves",
    "idea_suggested": "what small idea you proposed"
}
